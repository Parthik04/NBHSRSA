Zayed El Aaidy

Role: Vice President

School & Grade: Moncton High School, Grade 12

About: My name is Zayed El Aaidy, and I am a Grade 12 student at Moncton High School. I currently serve as the Vice President of the New Brunswick High Schools Racialized Student Alliance (NBHS-RSA) where I work to build an environment that includes and supports all high school students, regardless of their language, ethnicity, or background.

Born and raised in the United Arab Emirates and proud of my Moroccan roots, I grew up in a country where people from all over the world lived and worked together. Seeing that cultural unity molded my belief that unity is the cornerstone of a thriving society, and I am passionate about creating that exact sense of unity by using my voice to advocate for cultural representation and equity.

Through my involvement in the student council, I’ve gained firsthand experience in leadership, teamwork, and the dedication it takes to plan events. I also learned what it means to represent fellow students, manage responsibilities under pressure, and turn ideas into meaningful actions. That’s why the quote: “With great power, comes great responsibility.” is so important to me.

I joined NBHS-RSA to challenge the systems that prevent racialized students from reaching their full potential. Listening to their stories has deepened my resolve for advocacy. As Vice President, I use my leadership to promote cultural representation and raise awareness of the unique mental health challenges faced by racialized youth. I support our team and work closely with the President to help bring our vision to life.

Outside of the Alliance, I enjoy meeting new people and joining fun events and activities. In my free time, you’ll often find me hanging out with friends—usually on some random side quest.
