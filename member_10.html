Suzanne Shah

Role: Founder

School: St. Thomas University (3rd Year)

About: Chairing a province-wide student alliance is never just a title—it is a responsibility I carry with purpose. I, Suzanne Shah, am one of the founders and the Chair of the New Brunswick High School Racialized Student Alliance. Together, we lead a collective committed to amplifying the voices of racialized students across the province.

I oversee the direction of our work, facilitate meaningful conversations, and support student-led initiatives that challenge systemic inequities and center lived experiences.

Born and raised in India, I bring a deep understanding of cultural complexity, resilience, and the power of representation. This journey fuels my passion for advocacy and storytelling. I believe in journalism that doesn’t just report the news, but makes space for the unseen and unheard.

Currently in my third year at St. Thomas University, I study Journalism, Communications, and Digital Media—not just as academic subjects, but as tools for change and connection. My goal is to become the world’s first hijabi journalist at CBC, and someday, BBC. I am here to tell stories that matter—and to make sure the world listens.
